Scratch Users and the Community

The Scratch community consists of all people who use Scratch: kids, teachers, parents, adults, all united under one programming language and online environment. The community began a very small one in 2007 but has grown to an large number of users, turning into millions. There is a follow button for those who wish to receive updates about the user they will be following. Many users care only about the number of followers they have, although some others do not. Scratch consists of coders (the most common), animators, RPGers, and artists (mostly). Like many other websites, you can add comments to interact with one another. Scratch encourages positives comments and constructive critism - otherwise, you may risk getting banned.

Need more help?

Simply log onto Scratch's forums and ask your question there!
